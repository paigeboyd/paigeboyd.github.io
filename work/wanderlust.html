Wanderlust Icons

An illustrative set of icons depicting some popular landmarks scattered throughout Europe.

This project involved creating a cohesive icon set, illustrating from photographic resources and coding a website which allowed visitors to download the icons for personal use.

Tools used: Adobe Illustrator, HTML, CSS & JavaScript, Adobe After Effects.

The Brief

Part of a project during my university degree, we were tasked with designing and illustrating a set of icons and a website to go along with them. The aim of this module was to give an insight into the world of digital product design and the product and business models that come along with it.

This brief was relatively small which allowed me to have more creative freedom; the only requirements are that the icons must look cohesive and as part of a set, and the website should be single page, promote the icons and allow users to download them.

Research

Almost immediately I knew I wanted to create icons based on architecture. I thought about creating icons for famous landmarks, and eventually narrowed this down to European Landmarks. I decided on having a brand for the icons and the website, that the icons were going to be an illustrative style rather than lines, and that they would be free to download with the option for the user to share them on Twitter if they liked it.

I chose the landmarks at random; some I had visited and some were in popular search results on Google. The landmarks I chose are:

  • Big Ben, London
  • La Sagrada Família, Barcelona
  • Konzerthaus, Berlin
  • Halászbástya, Budapest
  • Prague Castle, Prague
  • Hallgrímskirkja, Reykjavik
  • Notre Dame, Paris
  • Fontana di Trevi, Rome

Wireframing

As always, I started out on paper. I spent quite a long time sketching out ideas for the icons; considering their shape and how the landmark would be contained within the shape.

I sketched a rough outline of each landmark in a dot grid notebook, which allowed me to ensure I was illustrating the icons at an even size. Tap the images below to enlarge.

I decided to create a day and night set, so I created gradients to reflect this and added these to the icon shape. I used my sketches and photographs of the landmarks to map out the shape using vector shapes in Illustrator, to keep it simple at the beginning. Once the shape of the landmark was created, I used the eyedropper tool to take colours from the photographs, and then moved back to the shapes and pen tools to create the finer details. I hand-lettered the 'Wanderlust' logo, and vectorised it in Illustrator also.

As these icons are quite detailed, I took each one in sections. Firstly, creating the background, then creating the overall shape of the landmark. The next step was adding in additional details; bricks, statues etc. The final step was adding in any background buildings to fill up some empty space and show the landmark's surroundings. Tap the images below to enlarge.

I decided to keep the website simple, using sections within the HTML to divide the website up. I used HTML and CSS for the layout and design of the website, and JavaScript to add interactions such as a smooth scroll and a switch which changed the icon sets from day to night.

Reflecting on this Project...

I am really pleased with how the icons came out. I wanted to spend a lot of time making them as detailed as possible so that it was obvious what the landmark was and I think I achieved this. I like the idea of the switch to change the icon sets from day to night.

I think I have managed to keep the icons quite consistent and of the same overall style, and the hand-lettered branding ties the sets together. I would love to continue adding to these as my illustration skills have improved a lot since beginning this project.

What can I improve on?

I could definitely improve on my hand-lettering skills. I need to improve on the kerning and keeping the letters on a baseline, but I know these skills will come with practice.
