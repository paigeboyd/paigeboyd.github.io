Danske Bank

I was asked to join Danske Bank's App Development team in Belfast as a Freelance Digital Designer. My role involved designing digital banking apps, ensuring that the user's needs were the centre of the design.

Mobile Lite

These days the majority of banking is done online or using an app. While this doesn't cost the millenial generation a second thought, for Danske's elderly or 'technophobe' customers, this is a daunting thought.
Mobile Lite is designed to allow the user to do basic, 'read-only' tasks; meaning they cannot make any changes or payments. This will help customers transition from banking in branch to hopefully using Danske Bank's Mobile Banking App.
I kept the UI light and used simple iconography to help users navigate through the app. Onboarding was key with this target audience, so I focused on creating clear, concise instructions. I also made use of Danske's secondary colour palette to bring a friendly vibe to the app.

Business Tracker

This is an internal product for Danske Bank. I designed Business Tracker; a tool to allow the business banking team to process applications easily.

This tool provides the team with the ability to track specific users' applications, view their status, view user information and approve or decline applications. It streamlines the process and makes it more efficient.

This tool was designed within Danske's Brand Guidelines. I used plenty of whitespace within the design to prevent the screen looking too overcrowded with information.

eBanking Sign Up

To reduce the need to visit a branch to create a bank account, Danske developed a 'sign up' app.
I placed a heavy focus on onboarding when designing this app to guide users through their application seamlessly. I created simple icons and illustrations to accompany any information on screen.
I stuck to Danske's primary colour palette to keep the brand evident and keep the user's trust throughout the app. These are two of the onboarding screens which explain what steps the user needs to take to complete the next part of the application. In the end, I created a fully interactive prototype, ready for user testing.
