Danske Bank

I was asked to join Danske Bank's App Development team in Belfast as a Freelance Digital Designer to help create digital banking apps, ensuring that the user's needs were the centre of the design.

Mobile Lite

The majority of banking is done online or using an app. For Danske's elderly or 'technophobe' customers, this is a daunting thought. Mobile Lite is designed to allow the user to do 'read-only' tasks, helping them transition to eBanking.
I kept the UI light with simple iconography to help users navigate the app. Onboarding was key with this audience, so I focused on creating concise instructions. I also made use of Danske's secondary colour palette to bring a friendly vibe to the app.
Tools used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe XD.

Business Tracker

This is an internal product for Danske Bank. I designed Business Tracker; a tool to allow the business banking team to process applications easily.

This tool provides the team with the ability to track specific users' applications, view their status, view user information and approve or decline applications. It streamlines the process and makes it more efficient.

Business Tracker was designed within Danske's Brand Guidelines, and I used plenty of whitespace within the design to prevent the screen looking too overcrowded with information. I created an interactive prototype to present for user testing.

Tools Used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe XD, Google Charts

eBanking Sign Up

To reduce the need to visit a branch to create a bank account, Danske developed a 'sign up' app.
I placed a heavy focus on onboarding to guide users through their application with simple illustrations to accompany any information. I stuck to Danske's colour palette to capture the user's trust.
Provided with a brief and user research, I created a fully interactive prototype, ready for external user testing.
Tools used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe XD.
